# Geopolitics Corner: Understanding the world around you

#### THE GREAT DISORDER

Since the beginning of this year President Trump has orchestrated a military assault in Venezuela, threatened to intervene in Colombia, Mexico, Cuba and again in Iran. He promised to annex Greenland, to downgrade Nato and EU. He dispatched thousands of federal agents to Minnesota and several other states and big cities, all ruled by Democrats; he launched, as a retribution, law suits agains the head of the Federal Reserve and other high ranking officials; he ignored the Congress, the Supreme Court and the Constitution.

Year 2026 was just at the end of its second month, when United Sates together with Israel (possibly the latter together with the former) started a war on Iran. The American democracy is in danger. And, accordingly, the world order.

_**Content and objectives**_

Our discussion will focus on:

- DONALD TRUMP – AN IMPERIAL FOREIGN POLICY
- MIDDLE EAST – THE FOREVER WARS
- UKRAINE & GREENLAND – EUROPE IN THE LABYRINTH
- ULTIMATE WEAPON – THE NUCLEAR ARMS RACE

The approach will be more journalistic than academic. Students are invited to participate actively in the course, whether in the form of answering the questions, interrupting with their own questions, or engaging in productive conversation, in order to:

- expand their knowledge on key geopolitical issues;
- develop their critical and analytical skills when reading the news;
- share their ideas and participate in active discussions.

### Ugo Tramballi

![](https://www.unibocconi.it/sites/default/files/styles/scale_64x64/public/media/images/ugo_tramballi.png.webp?itok=a1QVlKDz)

ISPI Senior Advisor in charge of the Institute’s India Desk.

Ugo Tramballi is a veteran journalist, he joined the Metropolitan section of “Il Giornale” in 1976. He served as the newspaper’s Middle East correspondent in Beirut from 1983 to ’87 with war correspondences from Lebanon, Iran, Iraq, Afghanistan and extensive coverage in India and Pakistan. He was Moscow correspondent from 1987 to ’91 and then Global Correspondent and columnist with the Italian Economic newspaper “Il Sole 24 Ore”. He is a member of the Istituto Affari Internazionali, IAI, Rome, as well as a World Economic Forum’s Media Leader. On ISPI’s website, he publishes a weekly blog on international affairs, “Slow News”.
