# Services for Students with Disabilities, SLDs, ADHD and Other Specific Needs

The University provides a range of measures and services to ensure equal opportunities for **students with disabilities**, **specific learning disorders (SLDs)**, **ADHD**, **and other specific needs**, supporting them throughout their entire academic journey.

![Student using computer tool for low vision](https://www.unibocconi.it/sites/default/files/styles/container_100_lg/public/media/images/_mg_5731.jpg.webp?itok=YwVpiMhZ)

## Students with Disabilities

Financial Aid

For students enrolled in [Bachelor and Law programs](https://www.unibocconi.it/en/applying-bocconi/bachelor-and-law-programs/funding/tuition-waiver-students-disabilities-equal-or-greater-66-ay-2026-27 "Tuition waiver for students with disabilities equal or greater than 66% - A.Y. 2026-27") or [Master of Science and MA programs](https://www.unibocconi.it/en/applying-bocconi/master-science-and-ma-programs/funding/tuition-waiver-students-disabilities-equal-or-greater-66-master-science-programs "Tuition waiver for students with disabilities equal or greater than 66% - Master of Science programs a.y. 2026-27"), a full tuition waiver is provided in case of 66% disability recognized throughout Italy or greater.

Housing

The University has provided accommodations in each [residence hall](https://www.unibocconi.it/en/applying-bocconi/bachelor-and-law-programs/housing/life-bocconi-dorms-ay-2026-27 "Life in the Bocconi dorms a.y. 2026-27") designed to assist students with disabilities. Those with 66% disability or greater have priority in the allocation of housing over other students.

Teaching and Exams

The University provides technologies that support classroom learning and individual study, including [Sensus Access](https://www.unibocconi.it/en/campus/services/sensus-access "Sensus Access") (for converting documents into accessible alternative formats) and the Blackboard Ally plug-in, in addition to textbooks in digital format.

Regarding exams, compensatory and dispensatory measures are assigned according to medical documentation, which may include extra time or the use of calculators or a computer.

For more information, see the _Compensatory/Dispensatory Measures During Exams: Initial Request, Types and Deadlines_ paragraph of the [Guides to the University](https://www.unibocconi.it/en/current-students/guide-university "Guide all'Università") _._

Library Services

The Library offers [dedicated services](https://www.unibocconi.it/en/current-students/library-archives/about-us/accessibility-and-inclusion "Accessibility and Inclusion") to guarantee access to information and to provide accessible spaces, as well as reserved priority seating. In addition, it is an institution authorized by the Italian Ministry of Culture to produce accessible digital copies of works protected by copyright for people with neurological, visual or motor disabilities.

Mobility Companion Service

A special [mobility companion service](https://www.unibocconi.it/en/about-us/diversity-equity-and-inclusion/equal-opportunities-and-belonging/mobility-companion-service-people-disabilities "Mobility Companion Service for People with Disabilities") is available to help people with disabilities move around campus.

Home-University Transportation

A [transfer service](https://www.unibocconi.it/en/about-us/diversity-equity-and-inclusion/equal-opportunities-and-belonging/home-university-transfer-people-motor-disabilities "Home-University Transfer for People with Motor Disabilities") provides home-university transport for students, staff and faculty with motor disabilities.

Service Animals

Properly trained [service animals](https://www.unibocconi.it/en/about-us/diversity-equity-and-inclusion/equal-opportunities-and-belonging/campus-access-service-animals "On-Campus Access of Service Animals") can accompany people with motor disabilities, sensory disabilities, psychiatric disorders or serious illnesses on campus.

International Exchange Programs

The Study Abroad office examines the specific needs of every student with disabilities who may be interested in participating in an [exchange program](https://www.unibocconi.it/en/international-mobility "International Mobility"), collaborating with partner universities to identify the available services in terms of accessibility and support.

Entering the Workforce

The [Disabilities and Work (DAL) Service](https://www.unibocconi.it/en/current-students/career-services/career-guidance-approaching-job-market/career-guidance "Career Guidance") supports people with disabilities when looking for internship and job opportunities. The Service organizes one-on-one counseling meetings, monitors application processes, and is the point of contact for employers seeking candidates in this category.

For more information, please visit the FAQ section on yoU@B > B in Touch > 'Inclusion, Disability and Wellbeing', where you can submit your inquiry.

You may also contact the [Inclusion Service](https://www.unibocconi.it/en/about-us/diversity-equity-and-inclusion/governance-people-and-policies/inclusion-service "Inclusion Service") office to request an interview and learn more about the available services.

## Students with SLDs, ADHD and Other Specific Needs

Teaching and Exams

The University provides technologies that support classroom learning and individual study, including [Sensus Access](https://www.unibocconi.it/en/campus/services/sensus-access "Sensus Access") (for converting documents into accessible alternative formats) and the Blackboard Ally plug-in, in addition to textbooks in digital format.

Regarding exams, compensatory and dispensatory measures are assigned according to medical documentation, which may include extra time or the use of calculators or a computer.

For more information, see the _Compensatory/Dispensatory Measures During Exams: Initial Request, Types and Deadlines_ paragraph of the [Guides to the University](https://www.unibocconi.it/en/current-students/guide-university "Guide all'Università") _._

Library Services

The Bocconi Library is an institution authorized by the Italian Ministry of Culture to [produce accessible copies](https://www.unibocconi.it/en/current-students/library-archives/about-us/accessibility-and-inclusion) of works protected by copyright for people with SLDs.

Learning Needs Help Desk

The [Desk](https://www.unibocconi.it/en/current-students/campus-life/learning-needs "Learning Needs") was established to provide every student with an SLD the chance to receive individual counseling on the best study strategies to tackle the university experience.

Peer to Peer Tutoring Project

This [service](https://www.unibocconi.it/en/current-students/campus-life/peer-peer-tutoring-project "Peer to Peer Tutoring Project") aims to improve and optimize study abilities through peer support, provided by student volunteers who have received specific training.

You may contact the Academic Specific Needs office via B in Touch > Exams > Disability and Specific Needs.
