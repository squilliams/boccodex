# Accessibility and Inclusion

## Dedicated services

- Access to the Library spaces
- Access to the first floor for students with limited mobility with personal badges from the alarmed door at the elevator exit

Availability of:

- 2 Reserved seats, exclusively for persons with disabilities
- 4 Priority seats, for non-exclusive use: available to all, upon reservation, but to be vacated if requested by persons with disabilities
- 1 dedicated workstation with a lowered desk dedicated to wheelchair users for online catalogue browsing
- Online delivery of accessible digital material for entitled users (users with disabilities or specific learning disorders) thanks to the fact that Bocconi University is recognized as “Entità autorizzata” by the Ministry of Culture under Law 633/1941 and the DPCM July 6, 2020. Here is a [list](https://www.unibocconi.it/sites/default/files/media/attachments/Catalogo%20inclusion_marzo%202026.pdf?VersionId=XyBjZp1x6oQKJh0RIO2Vwe4nr6.hUqHN "inclusion catalogue") of texts in accessible format from other “Entità autorizzate” and available by request
- [Sensus Access](https://www.unibocconi.eu/wps/wcm/connect/bocconi/sitopubblico_en/navigation+tree/home/campus+and+services/services+for/sensus+access): application for converting documents and web pages into accessible formats
- Campus Guide, created in partnership with Istituto dei Ciechi di Milano specifically for users with visual impairments, available at the Library Main Entrance
- Bocconi campus tactile model, available at the entrance to Via Gobbi 5 building

For other services offered by the University, please visit the following page [Services for Students with Disabilities, SLDs, ADHD and Other Specific Needs](https://www.unibocconi.it/en/current-students/inclusion-disabilities-and-learning-disorders/services-students-disabilities-slds-adhd-and-other-specific-needs "Services for Students with Disabilities, SLDs, ADHD and Other Specific Needs").

Here are also our Libguides:

- [Accessibilty and health libguide](https://unibocconi.libguides.com/accessibility): a selection of relevant resources and tools, available through the Library or freely accessible online, related to accessibility, learning disabilities and mental health
- [Diversity and inclusion libguide](https://unibocconi.libguides.com/diversity): related to gender, LGBTQIA+, geographic and ethnic background

If you need to get browsing from your device more accessible and easier, you can choose one of the free add-ons offered for [Google Chrome](https://chrome.google.com/webstore/category/collection/3p_accessibility_extensions) and [Mozilla Firefox](https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/search/?q=accessibility&type=extension). You can choose them also using the Library's computers in the reading rooms.

For further information and assistance: [infobiblio@unibocconi.it](mailto:infobiblio@unibocconi.it)
