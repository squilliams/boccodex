# Open Day

16 May 2026

9:00am - 5:00pm

On Campus and Online

Activities for High School Students

Join us and discover what it means to study at Bocconi

The Open Day event scheduled for **16 May 2026** is dedicated to **high school students** interested in learning more about Bocconi. You can attend program presentations to learn about the main updates for Bachelor programs, interact with faculty and students and get all the information you need to make the right choice about your university studies.

**Registration is required** to participate.

### Activities

- **Our University and Bachelor Programs**, introduction to Bocconi with the Rector, the Dean of the Undergraduate School and the Dean of the School of Law
- **Bachelor Programs**, presentations and Q&A with the Directors
- **Admissions**, presentations and Q&A with Bocconi staff
- **Fees, Funding and Housing,** presentations and Q&A with Bocconi staff
- **Bocconi Meets Sport Athletes**, presentation and Q&A with Bocconi staff
- **Desks** to get more information on services provided by the University
- **Student Lounge**, where you can meet Bocconi students and ask questions about course content and student life
- **Campus and Housing Tours**
- **Online simulation** of the admissions test

**Focus: Online simulation of the admissions test**

You can take an online simulation of the admissions test **on 20, 21 and 22 May 2026**.

If you're interested, you must select the admissions test option when completing your **registration for the Open Day event** and **choose your preferred day** among the three days. On Monday 18th May you'll receive an email with a **link** to access the simulation, along with your **log-in information**.

**Choose how you want to attend** and register!

**Don't wait**: a limited number of spots is available.

You can **change your selections** at any time by accessing the [**event platform**](https://bocconiopenday.liveforum.space/membership/login?lang=en-GB&source=programma) with the email and password entered during registration.

ONLINE

You will receive a confirmation email after registration. If you change your mind and would like to attend on campus, you can always change your registration - based on availability.

ON CAMPUS

When you register for the event, remember to select the Welcome Speech you would like to attend, the activities you're interested in participating in and whether you will be attending with a guest. **Access to presentation rooms is restricted** to participants who have reserved that activity. Only some activities on the day of the event will be open to everyone.

After completing registration, you will receive your personalized program with a **personal QR code** to access all the activities at the email address indicated on the form.

You can change your selections at any time by accessing the [**event platform**](https://bocconiopenday.liveforum.space/membership/login?lang=en-GB&source=programma). On 16 May you can also use the [**platform**](https://bocconiopenday.liveforum.space/membership/login?lang=en-GB&source=programma) to see your schedule, get your QR code and participate in the chat rooms.

NB: Each student can **attend the on-campus event with only one guest**(parent or other guest). Therefore, when completing the registration form, make sure you enter your guest's contact information so they receive an invitation to register for the event. They will then receive **their personal QR code** and be able to attend the activities you select for 16 May.

### A Sustainable Bocconi

Bocconi's foundation in 1902 was based on a set of ethical and responsible core values that have guided it to a robust and lasting focus on sustainability issues. This commitment is fueled through teaching and research, to best prepare our students and contribute to scientific and social progress. Bocconi also undertakes direct initiatives in many areas, including energy efficiency, saving resources, reducing the use of materials such as paper and plastic, and recycling waste.

We therefore aim to reduce the impact of the event on campus in the following ways:

- **Less paper**: Brochures and other materials will not be printed but rather available online or accessible through QR codes
- **Less plastic**: Water dispensers are available on campus where you can fill your water bottle at no cost
- **Less pollution**: We encourage you to come to campus using public transportation when possible.
