## Page 2

Before applying to the Exchange program, carefully read the following documents:

- ☑ Bocconi Students TRAVEL RISK POLICY
Available on yoU@B – International SOS

- ☑ Guide to the Exchange Program
Available on our website

In addition, please consider that:

- ☑ Awareness about countries’ safety and medical conditions is essential. Detailed information available on the International SOS service portal (yoU@B agenda - International Programs). The link to International SOS is also included in the Universities’ boxes (click on the “Travel &amp; Safety focus” link and search for the interested location in the drop-down menu on the left).

- ☑ In light of the general fluidity of the global situation, travel and medical risks can rapidly change imposing travel and medical restrictions. Bocconi and/or the partner universities may decide to cancel the program/s in order to safeguard the well-being and security of students.

- ☑ This brochure has been released in February 2025 and reflects the situation to date. Information about Partner Universities may be subject to change.

- ☑ Concerning slots available, requirements, restrictions, etc. only the information contained in this list will apply to the Exchange Program selection for the a.y. 2025-26.

Also, it is important to know that:

- ☑ During the exchange semester, students must comply with the rules, regulations, and codes of conduct established by the Host Institution.

- ☑ Students are responsible for covering living expenses, including rent, food, healthcare, recreation, travel and transportation, health insurance, textbooks, school supplies, visa documents, and other related costs. Please note that, some universities may require the payment of administrative fees for service fruition (check schools’ website/factsheet).

- ☑ Students must look for accommodation on their own or with the assistance of the host university. Solutions made available by the host universities abroad (if any) can vary. For additional information, check partner schools’ website/factsheet and previous exchange students' reports.

- ☑ Students can enroll in courses offered by the Host Institution, if not exclusively reserved for local students. However, it's important to note that enrollment in specific courses at the host institution is never guaranteed and depends on various factors, such as meeting prerequisites, seat availability, etc.

- ☑ International students or students with dual citizenship cannot apply for some Schools in the countries for which they hold current citizenship. Check the note section for each destination. In case of doubt please contact the Study Abroad office through B in Touch on the yoU@B Diary.
