# Selection and admissions process

The admission requirements and selection process for the MCF program are designed to ensure strong alignment between the program and your aspirations. This rigorous approach aims to meet your expectations while providing a foundation for personal and professional growth.

We select candidates from diverse academic, cultural, and professional backgrounds, as this diversity enriches the learning experience at both the individual and group levels.

Our selection process involves a holistic evaluation of your application materials, considering key factors such as skills, professional experience, personal drive, and leadership and growth potential. Through this process, we aim to create a class of outstanding individuals who will benefit from the program—not only to achieve their career goals and personal aspirations but also to enrich their peers' learning experience.

## Admissions results

As soon as the selection process is completed, you will receive a formal letter from the Admissions committee notifying you of the decision. Selection results are never communicated by telephone. All decisions made by the Admissions committee are final.

The selection process will result in one of the following outcomes:

- **Admission:** You will receive the official admission notice along with the participation contract. You must confirm your participation by the deadline specified by the Admissions committee.

If all available spots have been filled, the school may place you on a waiting list or offer admission for the following edition.

Admission to the program is valid for the year the application is submitted. However, you can request deferment to the following year, but approval of deferment is on a case-by-case decision by the Admissions committee.

- **Non-Admission:** Admission to the program is highly selective. Our selection process evaluates each candidate’s individual profile while also considering the overall composition of the MCF class, which must be consistent with the quality and diversity the school requires. While we acknowledge the significant effort all candidates invest in the application and the selection process, we do not provide personalized feedback to non-admitted candidates.

## How to apply

The application is available **exclusively online** and must be completed in English. The selection process begins in October 2025.

**Please note that the online application portal is a separate platform, so your SDA Bocconi website (www.sdabocconi.it) credentials cannot be used. Please proceed with a new registration.**

- All required documents must be uploaded to the relevant section of the **Online application portal** and submitted to the Admissions team.
- Applicants may track the receipt of their application and monitor their application status directly through the online application.
- Additional documents can be uploaded after the initial application submission.

We encourage you to apply early to maximize your chances for admission.

## We look for highly motivated candidates with well-defined goals

The application is available exclusively online and must be completed in English. The selection process begins on **October 2025**.

**Applications are examined on a rolling basis**. Those received well in advance of the deadline have a greater chance of being admitted. Therefore, it is in your best interest to submit your application as early as possible.

When we receive your application, we carry out a **pre-selection** based on the documents you sent. The Admissions Committee lets you know if you are considered suitable to proceed with the selection process.

If so, you will be required to have an **interview**, which may be held at SDA Bocconi in Milan or remotely (by phone or Microsoft Teams). This will allow you to show that you have the essential skills, talents, and motivations that are essential to getting the most out of our MCF program.

The **final result** will be communicated once the application is complete, which means all documents must have been received and tests completed. You will receive a formal letter from Admissions Service (results are never communicated via telephone).
