# Collaborative AI lab

The Collaborative AI Lab (CAIL) was established through a strategic partnership **between SDA Bocconi School of Management** and **the Department of Computing Sciences at Bocconi University**. The Lab aims to foster the convergence of expertise, stakeholders, and technologies to address complex problems through advanced scientific research and cutting-edge industrial applications.

CAIL promotes close and integrated collaboration between researchers and professionals from partner companies. Within a shared environment, **the Lab develops advanced AI research, leveraging high computational capacity while consistently safeguarding data privacy, security, and ownership**.

CAIL is part of Bocconi University’s broader education and research initiative on AI, in collaboration with Fondazione Compagnia di San Paolo.

## Mission

The mission of CAIL is to facilitate the convergence of:

- **Advanced research**: promoting scientific innovation in the most advanced fields of AI.
- **Cutting-edge technologies**: leveraging High Performance Computing (HPC) resources and state-of-the-art technologies.
- **Outstanding expertise**: integrating researchers, engineers, students, domain experts, and companies.
- **Academic and industrial interests**: translating fundamental research into real-world applications.
- **Multidisciplinary approaches**: bringing together computer science, data science, economics, ethics, law, and behavioral sciences.

## Highlight

The Collaborative AI Lab stands out for its ability to **bring together expertise, technologies, and perspectives from different worlds**. The Lab’s strength lies in the integration of scientific excellence and advanced industrial applications: researchers, students, and companies collaborate daily in a shared environment where ideas and data can converge securely and safely.

This is further enhanced by access to high-performance computing resources and the expertise of the Department of Computing Sciences at Bocconi University, enabling the Lab to **tackle complex problems with state-of-the-art tools**. The Lab’s approach is deeply multidisciplinary: artificial intelligence engages with economics, ethics, management, and other disciplines to develop **innovative, responsible, and truly applicable solutions**.

## Objectives

CAIL was founded with the ambition of contributing to the advancement of artificial intelligence both scientifically and in terms of applications. Its primary objective is to promote **cutting-edge research spanning from fundamental AI to scalable solutions for businesses, creating a strong bridge between theory and practice**.

The Lab also seeks **to strengthen the role of Europe and Italy** in the global AI competition, offering an environment in which universities and companies can cooperate to generate sustainable innovation. At the same time, CAIL positions itself **as a hub for attracting and developing top talent in the field**, fostering growth paths that support a new generation of specialists, researchers, and technology leaders.

Finally, through partnerships, events, and outreach activities, the Lab aims to create **an ecosystem capable of disseminating knowledge, strengthening skills, and generating a positive impact on businesses, institutions, and society**.

## Audience and target

The Collaborative AI Lab addresses a broad and diverse range of stakeholders. **Companies seeking to better understand the potential of AI or to develop advanced applications** find in the Lab a scientific and operational partner with whom to explore new opportunities.

At the same time, **managers, professionals, and policymakers interested in AI, security, and governance** can access up-to-date content, informational initiatives, and tools to navigate a rapidly evolving technological landscape.

In this way, the Lab creates a meeting place for anyone wishing to contribute to the development of advanced, responsible, and socially beneficial artificial intelligence.

## Activities

- Research areas

CAIL’s research focuses on:

  - Deep learning, reinforcement learning, generative AI.
  - Security, privacy, and cryptography applied to AI.
  - HPC technologies for AI.
  - Cross-industry AI applications.
  - Methods for responsible and trustworthy AI.

- Special initiatives

  - Customized and pilot projects with participating companies.
  - Development of advanced privacy and security protocols.
  - Cryptography-for-AI initiatives conducted with the Department of Computing Sciences.

- Events

**• Research Talks** throughout the year on cutting-edge topics.

**• Presentations of results** from applied projects and tailored initiatives.

**• Annual Event** at SDA Bocconi to share developments, perspectives, and build a national AI community.

## Output

The Lab produces:

- Results in fundamental AI research.
- Scientific papers and publications.
- Application solutions and prototypes for companies.
- Reports and deliverables related to tailored initiatives.
- Contributions on security, privacy, cryptography, and responsible AI.
