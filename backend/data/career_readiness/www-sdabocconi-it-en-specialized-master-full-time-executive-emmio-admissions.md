# Selection and admission process

The admission requirements and selection process for the EMMIO program are designed to ensure a strong alignment between the program and your specific aspirations and professional goals.

To ensure a high-quality educational experience, SDA Bocconi applies a rigorous selection process based on clearly defined criteria.

We select candidates from diverse academic, cultural, and professional backgrounds, as this diversity enriches the learning experience for all.

In reviewing your application, we take a holistic approach—assessing your academic and professional history alongside your motivation, personal drive, and alignment with the program. We aim to ensure that EMMIO is the right match for you and that it supports your goals, expectations, and career aspirations.

Each year, this careful selection process builds a dynamic, international cohort of experienced professionals ready to tackle managerial challenges in international organizations and make an immediate impact in their work environment.

## Admissions results

As soon as the selection process is completed, you will receive a formal letter from the Admissions committee notifying you of the decision. Selection results are never communicated by telephone. All decisions made by the Admissions committee are final.

The selection process will result in one of the following outcomes:

- **Admission**: You will receive the official admission notice along with the participation contract. You must confirm your participation by the deadline specified by the Admissions committee.

If all available spots have been filled, the school may place you on a waiting list or offer admission for the following edition.

Admission to the program is valid for the year the application is submitted. However, you can request deferment to the following year, but approval of deferment is on a case-by-case decision by the Admissions committee.

- **Non-admission**: Admission to the program is highly selective. Our selection process evaluates each candidate’s individual profile while also considering the overall composition of the EMMIO class, which must be consistent with the quality and diversity the school requires. While we acknowledge the significant effort all candidates invest in the application and the selection process, we do not provide personalized feedback to non-admitted candidates.

## How to apply

The application form is available **exclusively online** and must be completed with accurate and precise information regarding your personal and professional experiences and your reasons for attending the program.

**Please note that the online application portal is a separate platform, so your SDA Bocconi website credentials cannot be used. Please proceed with a new registration.**

- All required documents must be uploaded to the relevant section of the Applicaton portaland submitted to the Admissions team.
- Applicants may track the receipt of their application and monitor their application status directly through the online application.
- Additional documents can be uploaded after the initial application submission.

We encourage you to apply early to maximize your chances for admission.

## We look for highly motivated candidates with well-defined goals

The application form is available exclusively online and must be completed in English. **The selection process begins in** **December** **2026\. Final application deadline: 16 July 2026.**

Applications are examined on a rolling basis. Those received well in advance of the deadline have a greater chance of being admitted. Therefore, it is in your best interest to submit your application as early as possible.

Only submitted applications will be considered for the selection process.

**Enrollment**

Upon confirmation of your admission, you will receive an enrollment form that must be completed, signed and uploaded to Application portal by the deadline indicated in the admission letter. To complete the enrollment process, you have to make the first tuition payment.

After enrolling, some international students (especially from outside the EU), may also be required to send by land mail the official/ original or certified degree certificate/ transcript.
