# Fees and scholarships

**A challenging but high-return investment**

Participation in the DBA program requires a substantial financial commitment. However, it is a strategic investment that delivers high future returns—both personally and professionally.

## Fees and expenses

The fee for the entire DBA program is 96,000 EUR payable in three installments as follows:

- Academic year 1

**€32,000**

- Academic year 2

**€32,000**

- Academic year 3

**€32,000**

**Application fee: €120**

The fees include all academic materials, use of the Bocconi and SDA Bocconi libraries and online resources, lunches and coffee breaks during residential modules.

Candidates are responsible for covering their travel and lodging expenses while in Milan.

## Scholarships and tuition waivers

_1st round_
26 March 2026

_2nd round_
28 May 2026

Each year, SDA Bocconi School of Management makes a number of merit-based scholarships and tuition waivers available for candidates with outstanding profiles. Admission to the program is independent of the awarding of any scholarship or tuition waiver.

For candidates admitted to the DBA 2026-2029, SDA Bocconi offers **seven "Outstanding Candidates Merit-Based Tuition Waivers" of up to €32,000 (33% of tuition fees).**

**The waivers are awarded in two rounds:**

- **1st Round: 3 waivers** for candidates who apply by the first deadline of **26 March 2026**
- **2nd Round: 4 waivers** for applications submitted by the final deadline of **28 May 2026**(including those submitted by the first deadline of 26 March).

To be considered for a tuition waiver in either round, candidates must both select the tuition waiver option in the DBA application and submit the application itself by the corresponding deadline listed above.

**Applications are evaluated according to the following criteria:**

- Analysis of the requirements indicated in the specific notice of competition
- Analysis of academic and professional record
- Results of the DBA selection process

## Loans and funding

SDA Bocconi provides opportunities for low-interest loans to admitted students, thanks to agreements with Italian Banks for [**residents of Italy**](https://www.unibocconi.it/en/programs/current-students/student-loans). Loan applications to Italian Banks must be submitted by the end of month that follows your Master starting date.

Furthermore, the University provides [**international students**](https://www.unibocconi.it/en/programs/current-students/funding-country/) with information about possible sources for financing in several countries. For further information, please visit the Università Bocconi website.
