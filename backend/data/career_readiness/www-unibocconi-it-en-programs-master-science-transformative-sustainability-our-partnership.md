# Our Partnership

Explore the Program

[Overview](https://www.unibocconi.it/en/programs/master-science/transformative-sustainability) [Study Plan](https://www.unibocconi.it/en/programs/master-science/transformative-sustainability/study-plan) [Career Opportunities](https://www.unibocconi.it/en/programs/master-science/transformative-sustainability/career-opportunities) [Program Placement](https://www.unibocconi.it/en/programs/master-science/transformative-sustainability/program-placement)

The **Bocconi University-Politecnico di Milano partnership** aims to best meet the new needs of the job market as companies and organizations increasingly transition toward sustainability. It is intended to reduce the gap between supply and demand by offering a program that can benefit from the distinctive specializations of both Universities.

The program will prepare Sustainability professionals with inter-disciplinary and hybrid skills to **face the complex challenges of environmental and social sustainability**. Technology must be guided by an innovation process for sustainability that starts from the vision and objectives to be achieved and takes into account all the variables that characterize new business models. A **joint initiative adds value by complementing the key competence areas** offered by the two Universities. The MSc offers a novel mix of skills and disciplines not currently available and targets a different market need.

Continuing a longstanding collaboration, two of Italy’s most prestigious universities, **Bocconi** and [**Politecnico di Milano**](https://www.polimi.it/en/), have once again joined forces. The joint MSc in Transformative Sustainability aims to prepare a **new category of professionals in the field of sustainability**.

The two-year program is based on a complementary blend of their strengths: Politecnico brings knowledge on the most advanced technologies and an engineering approach to design, innovate and manage sustainable systems, while Bocconi shares its expertise on the strategic and economic fronts.

The result is an interdisciplinary and well-balanced foundation followed by deeper technical or managerial specialization.
