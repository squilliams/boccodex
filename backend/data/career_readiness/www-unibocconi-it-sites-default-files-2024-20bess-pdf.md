## Document summary

## Methodology

The survey on graduates' satisfaction was conducted by Bocconi University's QA & Accreditation Office in 2024. It targeted graduates from the Bachelor in Economic and Social Sciences program who completed their degrees in 2022 (1 year after graduation), 2020 (3 years after graduation), and 2018 (5 years after graduation). The survey aimed to gather opinions on various aspects of their educational experience and its impact on their careers.

## Satisfaction Indicators

The survey included several questions to gauge the satisfaction of graduates with their educational experience and its impact on their careers. Key findings include:

1. **Enrollment Decision**:
   - 54.2% of respondents would enroll in the same program and at the same university again.
   - 27.1% would enroll in a different program at a different university.

2. **Satisfaction with Education**:
   - 91% of respondents were satisfied with the knowledge, skills, and abilities acquired during their study program.
   - 73.6% found the program useful for applying theories in the field.
   - 80% were satisfied with their independent judgment skills.
   - 69.1% were satisfied with their communication skills.
   - 90.9% were satisfied with their learning skills.

3. **Job Consistency**:
   - 41.2% of respondents felt their current job was consistent with their studies.

4. **Degree Usefulness**:
   - 77.1% of respondents found their degree useful in their work, either as a requirement by law or as a necessary or useful qualification.

5. **Use of Acquired Knowledge**:
   - 61.1% of respondents use the knowledge, skills, and abilities acquired at university either definitely or more yes than no.

6. **Internship Facilitation**:
   - 63.7% of respondents who completed an internship felt it facilitated their placement in the job market.

7. **Study Abroad Facilitation**:
   - 27.3% of respondents who studied abroad felt it facilitated their placement in the job market.

# INTRODUCTION

This statistical report summarizes the opinions expressed by graduates in response to satisfaction surveys carried out 1, 3 and 5 years after graduation.

The data reported refer to graduates who completed the Degree Program in the years 2022, 2020 and 2018, respectively, and who did not continue their studies at Bocconi.

In particular, the opinions gathered concern:

- satisfaction with the Program and the set of knowledge and skills acquired at the end of it;
- the perception of the usefulness of the Program for the performance of one's job;
- impressions of the contribution provided by internship and study experiences abroad (if applicable) for the job placement.
