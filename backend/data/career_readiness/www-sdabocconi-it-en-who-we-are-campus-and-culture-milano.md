# Milano

## A Campus designed for the future

Our Milan campus is our home – a place where architecture, sustainability, and innovation merge to create a truly unique learning experience.

Designed by Japanese firm SANAA, it transformed a former industrial area (the Centrale del Latte) into a modern hub open to the city.

Six fluid, transparent buildings host classrooms, offices, housing for over 300 students, and a sports center with an Olympic swimming pool, gyms, a running track, and sports fields.

Sustainability is built into every detail, with architectural solutions that maximize energy efficiency and the well-being of those who live and study on campus.

## A tangible commitment to sustainability

The Milan campus is the most sustainable business school campus in the world, according to the Financial Times Carbon Footprint Rank – a recognition of our commitment to achieving carbon neutrality by 2025.

With over 1.2 MW of installed renewable energy from solar and geothermal sources, and 100% certified renewable electricity since 2017, we’ve turned sustainability into measurable action.

The campus was awarded LEED Platinum certification – the highest global standard for sustainable buildings – thanks to a design approach focused on reducing environmental impact and improving quality of life.
