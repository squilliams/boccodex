SUR Lab featured in Il Sole 24 Ore and Radiocor: measuring impacts to steer urban regeneration\\
\\
SUR Lab’s contribution to _Il Sole 24 Ore_ examines impact measurement as a key challenge in urban regeneration. It introduces the SUR Atlas, a repository of over 60 global case studies showing strong environmental, social, and economic outcomes. Tania Molteni highlights the lack of a shared urban-scale framework. The ESG Urban Track, developed within MUSA, is proposed to address this gap.](https://surlab.unibocconi.eu/news-events/sur-lab-featured-il-sole-24-ore-and-radiocor-measuring-impacts-steer-urban-regeneration)

Housing as a right, not a privilege: Book presentation on Italy’s housing crisis\\
\\
On Monday, 13 April, Bocconi University hosted the presentation of _Right or privilege? Housing needs and local housing policies_, a volume exploring Italy’s housing crisis through the lenses of inequality, welfare and local governance. The discussion with the authors and invited discussants highlighted how housing can no longer be treated as a marginal issue, but as a structural dimension of social justice and urban sustainability. The event, organised in collaboration with IFEL and INAPP, offered a timely opportunity to reflect on how fairer and more inclusive cities can be built.](https://surlab.unibocconi.eu/news-events/housing-right-not-privilege-book-presentation-italys-housing-crisis)

Housing: right or privilege? A public debate in Milan on housing policies\\
\\
A **public event** in Milan will explore Italy's growing **housing crisis** by presenting the book _“Housing: right or privilege?”_. Bringing together academics and practitioners, the discussion will address inequalities, urban transformation, and policy responses. The debate will be coordinated by Prof. **Edoardo Croci,** SUR Lab Director at Bocconi University.](https://surlab.unibocconi.eu/news-events/housing-right-or-privilege-public-debate-milan-housing-policies)

Planning Urban Development: The Madrid Plan – A New Episode of “Città in transizione”\\

The **third episode** of _Cities in Transition_ takes listeners to Madrid, where the Madrid Río project transformed a traffic-dominated riverfront into a vibrant, climate-resilient public space.
