# Master in Sustainability Management

## Management meets purpose.

Sustainability, diversity and inclusion

_Start_ January 2027

_Duration_ 12 months

_Where_ Milano

_Format_ Full-time

_Language_ English

_Avg. working experience_ 3 years

_Credits_ 70 ECTS/CFU

**Climate change, biodiversity loss, energy transitions.**

These aren’t just headlines. They’re the defining challenges of our time. While global political debates may shift perspectives, businesses, organizations, and civil society continue to treat sustainability as a priority. The need for skilled professionals who can drive real solutions remains stronger than ever.

The **Master in Sustainability Management (MSM) is your chance** to be part of the solution. This intensive, one-year program **(70 ECTS credits)**, prepares you to tackle sustainability from every angle ‒ business, policy, and innovation.

Whether you aim to work in industry, services, international agencies, nonprofits, or the public sector, MSM gives you the tools to make an impact where it matters most.

## Who is it for

The Master in Sustainability Management is right for you if:

**You want to** be more than just a professional ‒ you want to drive the shift toward a sustainable future.

**You see** your career on a global stage, working in sustainability, energy, or innovation with top organizations across public and private sectors.

**You believe** that climate change and sustainability aren’t just challenges ‒ they’re opportunities to reshape economies and societies.

**You’re looking for** a program that blends theory with real-world practice, all in just one year.

**You value** earning an internationally recognized qualification from one of the world’s most prestigious business schools, alongside a diverse, global cohort.

**You aim** to master the economic, regulatory, and managerial aspects of environmental and energy issues through a hands-on, well-rounded learning experience.

If you see yourself in these statements, this is your next step.

## Why MSM

- ### Shape the future of business with sustainability

This Master isn’t just about learning ‒ it’s about leading. You'll gain deep expertise in corporate sustainability, green management, sustainable supply chains, energy markets, finance, and advanced management techniques, while integrating environmental and social sustainability into strategic decision-making. In a world where sustainability is no longer optional, you’ll be ready to drive change across organizations, creating value that is economic, environmental and social in fast-moving, global industries.

- ### Learn from the best

Our faculty isn’t just world-class ‒ they’re game-changers. Thought leaders in sustainability, energy, and climate change, they collaborate with top organizations worldwide. Their research and expertise fuel real impact, making our program a launchpad for future leaders.

- ### An immersive, hands-on experience

This full-time program (January–December) blends 450+ hours of coursework with real-world experience: an internship (320-480 hours), workshops, field projects, executive seminars, and company insights. Whether you’re starting fresh or pivoting your career, you’ll gain the skills, network, and mindset to make sustainability your competitive edge.

- ### Management meeting purpose

Management fundamentals are never neutral: they are tools to drive meaningful impact. During your journey you’ll master strategy, finance and operations while learning to anchor every decision to a cause, a responsibility, a broader vision. The goal is to shape purpose-driven leaders who can create value in any industry, aligning performance with impact and business with meaning.

- ### Partners that move the world forward

Our network isn’t just global ‒ it’s influential. SDA Bocconi collaborates with top international organizations leading the charge in sustainability and energy. These partnerships open doors for recruitment, research, and real-world projects, placing you at the intersection of learning and action.

- ### Learn from those who lead

Why learn about sustainability only from a textbook when you can hear it firsthand? Throughout the program, top executives from global organizations share how they turned sustainability challenges into opportunities. Their stories aren’t just inspiring ‒ they’re roadmaps to the kind of leader you can become.

#### Preparatory courses: level playing field

To prepare for the MSM in-class activities, we recommend that those without an adequate academic background in economics, management or finance attend our online tutorials on **Economics**, **Accounting** and **Data Analysis**.

#### Core modules

This phase of MSM consists of **9 in-class compulsory courses**, **1 seminar** and **1 compulsory workshop** in green economy with the purpose of providing the fundamental knowledge and skills to tackle issues related to sustainability and energy challenges.

February 2027November 2027

#### Field projects

Field projects are a key part of the MSM learning experience, allowing students to **mimic real-life situations and work on practical assignments** as part of their compulsory class activities. MSM field projects are short-term assignments to be carried out in close cooperation with a company or any other organization interested in taking advantage of the training provided by this specialized program. Field projects will be **organized with leading global organizations** in the fields of corporate sustainability, energy and environment.

May 2027October 2027

#### Specialization modules: concentration track

There are three specialization tracks:

**• SUSTAINABLE ENERGY TRANSITION**

**• SUSTAINABILITY MANAGEMENT AND STRATEGIES**

**• SUSTAINABLE VALUE CREATION AND FINANCE (SCF)**

Each track includes four specialized courses aiming at further training students in a particular subarea of sustainability depending on their professional ambitions. Each student must **choose one track** at the beginning of the master and then attend all four relevant courses; furthermore each student will have to attend **two more specialized courses from the other tracks**.

September 2027December 2027

#### Final project (alternative options)

In the last phase students can opt for **either a in-company experience**, for a minimum of 12 weeks **or** for **research project**. The in-company experience and research project will be tutored by the Master’s faculty members.

## A comprehensive learning experience

- ### Learning environment

MSM integrates an intensive face-to-face teaching approach, essential for engaging with faculty and guest speakers, fostering participant interaction, and ensuring effective group dynamics. The program also includes self-directed learning moments such as case studies, group projects, virtual teamwork, simulations and experiential learning.

- ### A balance between theory and practice

The program combines in-class lectures, case study analysis, simulations and role playing, individual and group exercises, offering a well-rounded balance between theoretical learning and practical application.

- ### Faculty and industry experts

The program is led by SDA Bocconi faculty and enriched by contributions from managers, entrepreneurs, and business leaders, offering valuable professional and personal perspectives to enhance the learning experience.

- ### Leaders in sustainability series

Senior executives from Italian and international organizations will deliver seminars, sharing experience and insights in sustainability to enrich students’ knowledge and connect theory with real-world practice.

## Admissions

We seek highly motivated candidates with clear career goals and a commitment to excellence.

Our rigorous selection process ensures a dynamic, diverse, and high-achieving MSM class each year, ready to tackle sustainability from every angle ‒ business, policy, and innovation.

Our goal is to ensure that this challenging yet rewarding journey aligns with your aspirations.

**Requirements**:

- Degree certificate (in any discipline), transcript of the grades
- Application form
- An average of 3 years' work experience, detailed CV
- Proficiency in English
- GMAT/GRE or SDA test
- Two references
- Interview with the Program Director/the Admissions Committee
